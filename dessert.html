Dabo Kolo:

Recipe: Mix flour, sugar, baking powder, and cardamom in a bowl. Gradually add water and knead until you have a smooth dough. Roll the dough into small balls and deep-fry until golden brown and crispy. Sprinkle with powdered sugar before serving.

  • Tips: You can add a touch of cinnamon or nutmeg to the dough for extra flavor.
  • Flavors: Cardamom, cinnamon, nutmeg.

Atayef:

Recipe: Prepare a pancake-like batter with flour, semolina, yeast, sugar, water, and a pinch of salt. Let the batter rest for about an hour. Heat a non-stick pan and pour small portions of the batter to make small, round pancakes. Once cooked, fill each pancake with a mixture of sweetened ricotta cheese, nuts, and a hint of rosewater. Fold in half and serve.

  • Tips: You can drizzle the filled pancakes with a syrup made from sugar, water, and lemon juice for added sweetness.
  • Flavors: Rosewater.

Tizita Cake:

Recipe: Cream together unsalted butter and sugar until light and fluffy. Add eggs, vanilla extract, and cardamom. In a separate bowl, combine flour, baking powder, and a pinch of salt. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the butter mixture, alternating with milk. Pour the batter into a greased cake pan and bake until golden and a toothpick comes out clean. Allow to cool before serving.

  • Tips: You can dust the cake with powdered sugar or frost it with a cream cheese icing for added sweetness.
  • Flavors: Cardamom, vanilla.

Ambasha:

Recipe: In a mixing bowl, combine flour, sugar, baking powder, salt, and cardamom. Gradually add warm milk and knead the dough until smooth and elastic. Let the dough rise in a warm place until it doubles in size. Punch down the dough, shape it into a round loaf or individual rolls, and place it on a greased baking sheet. Brush the top with an egg wash and sprinkle with sesame seeds. Bake in a preheated oven until golden brown and cooked through. Allow it to cool before serving.

  • Tips: You can sprinkle the loaf with sesame seeds before baking for added texture.
  • Flavors: Cardamom.

Sambusa:

Recipe: Prepare a sweet filling by combining ingredients such as finely chopped apples or bananas, sugar, cinnamon, and a pinch of nutmeg. Fill small pastry sheets or wrappers with the sweet mixture, folding them into triangular shapes. Deep-fry the filled pastries until golden brown and crispy. Serve warm as a sweet treat.

  • Tips: To enhance the sweetness and flavor of the filling, you can add ingredients like chopped nuts (such as almonds or walnuts), raisins, or shredded coconut. Sprinkle powdered sugar or drizzle honey over the fried sambusas for added sweetness and presentation.
  • Spices: Cinnamon, nutmeg (optional).

Qoqor:

Recipe: Toast bread slices. Mash ripe avocado with a fork and spread it on the toast. Sprinkle with salt, pepper, and a squeeze of lemon juice. Optional: Top with sliced tomatoes, feta cheese, or a poached egg.

  • Tips: For added texture and flavor, you can incorporate chopped dried fruits (such as dates or apricots) or nuts (such as pistachios or cashews) into the dough. You can also brush the baked qoqor with a simple syrup made from sugar and water while it's still warm to add a sweet glaze.
  • Spices:Salt, pepper, red pepper flakes.
